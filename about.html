About Me

I'm a Materials Science and Engineering student at Northwestern University with a passion for innovation at the intersection of materials, electronics, and textiles.

My journey in technology began in high school when I first started coding. Since then, I've expanded my skills to include electronics development through my work with Formula SAE, where I help design and implement electronic systems for racing vehicles.

One of my primary research interests is the integration of electronics into fabrics, combining my technical knowledge with my love for sewing and textile crafts. I believe that e-textiles represent an exciting frontier in wearable technology with applications ranging from healthcare to fashion.

Currently, I'm conducting research with the Spin Lab at Northwestern, focusing on ethyl cellulose and its potential applications in sustainable materials and technologies.
